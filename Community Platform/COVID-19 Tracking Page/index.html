About CVID-19


The novel coronavirus (COVID-19) virus is an extremely infectious disease caused by new strain of Coronavirus.

Most people (mostly younger) infected with the COVID-19 virus will experience mild to moderate symptoms such as coughing, sore throat, and fever. Most often recover without requiring the use of medical treatment. However, older people, seniors, and those with pre-existing medical problems like chronic respiratory disease and/or cancer are more likely to develop serious cases than those without.

The most effective way to prevent and slow the spread of the virus is be informed about COVID-19, the symptons of the virus, how it spreads, and safe sanitation practices. Protect yourself and others in the Bellmare community from infection by washing your hands thoroughly and wearing a mask. Remember to limit touching one's facial features such as noses, mouths, eyes, or any other possible transmissible routes of the virus.

COVID-19 spreads mainly through droplets of saliva or discharge from the nose when an infected person coughs or sneezes, so it is extremely important that you practice respiratory etiquette (i.e wearing a mask when coughing) and social distancing when out in public.

As of today, there are no vaccines or medical treatments for the coronavirus. However, many lab groups and medical facilities are in the process of developing effective treatments for this virus, which includes the use of clinical trials on human subjects. The WHO and other news outlets will continue to provide updated information on the latest developments.
